Understanding Anxiety in the Digital Age

By Dr. Sarah Jenkins • October 12, 2023 • 5 min read

In an era where our smartphones are the last things we see before bed and the first things we reach for in the morning, our nervous systems are under unprecedented strain. The constant barrage of notifications, news alerts, and social media updates has created a pervasive background radiation of anxiety for many.

The Mechanics of Digital Stress

Every time your phone buzzes or pings, your brain releases a small spike of cortisol—the stress hormone. When this happens dozens or hundreds of times a day, your body is kept in a low-grade state of "fight or flight". This chronic arousal can lead to fatigue, irritability, and a feeling of being constantly overwhelmed.

Furthermore, social media platforms are specifically engineered using variable reward schedules—the exact psychological mechanism that makes slot machines addictive. We keep pulling to refresh, hoping for a dopamine hit in the form of a like, comment, or interesting piece of news.

"The modern mind is drowning in information while starving for wisdom."

Strategies for Reclaiming Your Attention

Fortunately, you are not powerless against the draw of your devices. By implementing structural boundaries, you can begin to down-regulate your nervous system.

  • Establish Tech-Free Zones: Define the bedroom and the dining table as areas where devices are simply not allowed. This naturally creates breaks and encourages present-moment awareness.
  • Batch Processing: Instead of checking email or messages continuously, designate specific times of day (e.g., 9 AM, 12 PM, and 4 PM) for checking and responding.
  • Curate Your Feed: Vigorously unfollow accounts that make you feel anxious or inadequate. Your social media feed should be a tool that serves you, not a stressor.
